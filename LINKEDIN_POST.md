What if your grandmother could file a civic complaint just by taking a photo?

Not fill a form. Not stand in a queue. Not speak English.

Just point, click, done.

That's what I built this weekend at #PromptWars — solo, in ~4 hours.

Meet Saathi — an AI civic companion for every Indian citizen. 🇮🇳

We talk a lot about "AI for good." I wanted to actually ship it — for the people who get left behind by every app: rural, elderly, low-literacy, and non-English speakers.

One companion. Six ways it helps 👇

💬 Baat Karo — chat with the government in your own language. Ask anything, get it in plain words.
📸 Samasya — snap a pothole or broken streetlight. AI classifies it, rates severity, routes it to the right municipal department, drafts a bilingual complaint + gives you a tracking ID.
📋 Yojana — describe your life in one line. AI finds the welfare schemes you're actually eligible for — rupee amounts and documents included.
📄 Kaagaz — upload a confusing government letter. Get a plain-language explanation + a checklist, read aloud.
🔎 Track — follow your complaint: Filed → Acknowledged → In Progress → Resolved.
🗣️ Multilingual — Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Marathi, Telugu, English. Your language flows into every single AI response.

The whole point: transparency and dignity shouldn't require a degree or a data plan.

Under the hood 🛠️
▪️ Next.js + TypeScript + Tailwind
▪️ Alibaba Cloud Qwen via DashScope — qwen-max for reasoning, qwen-vl-max for vision
▪️ Firebase for auth
▪️ Shipped on Vercel

Four hours. One developer. A civic companion that actually runs. 🚀
